Workflow

Works

Collection of bibliographic information

Our choice of literary texts was: Prometheus Bound and Trojan Women by Aeschylus, Agamemnon and Medea by Euripides, based on the criteria of linguistic and content variety and presence in our bibliographic sources. We asked ourselves: In which moments and contexts men turn to divine entities in Greek tragedies? / In which forms do they do it? / What kind of divine interventions is it possible to classify? / Is man impotent when he has to face divine entities? / In which cases human will and actions coincide with those of divine entities? / In which cases is it possible to recognize a similarity between a man and a divine entity?

Meeting with the domain expert

Meeting with Prof. Neri, expert of Ancient Greek language and literature, helped us shed light of which edition were better to take into account for our analysis, and suggested us some specific topics to focus on.

NLP and statistical analysis

To answer to our competency questions, we decided to use a wide variety of methods. In particular, we chose to exploit a combination of algorithms created in R and Python, along with the tool "VoyantTools" (voyant-tools.org) and the bibliographic study on the tragedies themselves.

Knowledge identification

Thanks to the linguistic analysis, it was easy to decide which were the most important concepts to convert into classes and object properties.

Knowledge extraction: the ontology

The ontology has then been created with Protege.
